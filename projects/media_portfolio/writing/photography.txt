Image_01 — Tranquility

Image 01 relates to atmosphere and the way media can shape how a moment feels. The trees, light, and density of the space create a sense of stillness that feels removed from distraction. It reflects the way media can frame an experience and make you pay attention to something you may have otherwise walked past. A lot of this class was about becoming more aware of how media shapes the way we interact with the world around us, and this image captures that for me. It is quiet, simple, and grounded, but that simplicity carries weight.

Image_02 — Craftsmanship

Image 02 connects to process and materiality. It shows the tools behind the work rather than the finished product. A lot of media focuses on final outcomes, but process carries its own narrative and weight. This image captures a moment of making and shows the relationship between the hand, the tool, and the material. As someone who works with physical materials often, this relates to the idea that the process is just as important as the result. The scattered tools reflect the reality of making something by hand.

Image_03 — A New Day

Image 03 is captured in the earliest hours of the day when first light peers over the mountains. I have always been a morning person and have always felt that the early hours are the best part of the day. It is the most peaceful, clear, and productive. Before the world fully wakes up there is space to think and work without distraction. This image captures that feeling. Media has the ability to preserve moments like this that are temporary by nature. Within minutes the light and atmosphere would be completely different. Capturing that moment gives permanence to something that would otherwise be gone.

Growth Reflection

This semester has helped me to start having a refined eye for how to capture environments and architecture. I started paying closer attention to framing and lighting and what the image was actually saying. As someone studying architecture this became more important because architecture is experienced through atmosphere, material, and light. A strong image can communicate those things clearly. I think my growth came from slowing down and observing more before taking the photo. Instead of just documenting what was in front of me I started trying to capture how a space felt. That shift changed the way I approach photography and made me more aware of how images shape the way people understand space and environment.
