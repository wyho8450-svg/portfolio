Media is the umbrella term that shelters all forms of communication and expression, whether it is something as old as a drawing utensil or something as new as an interactive digital platform.

What separates new media from old media most clearly is accessibility. Old media often had to be sought out and consumed intentionally, while new media is immediate and constantly within reach. It exists in the spaces we already occupy and asks less of us to engage with it.That accessibility can also be damaging. Constant exposure to media can affect mental health through comparison, overstimulation, and the inability to disconnect. Mental health and physical health are directly connected, so when one suffers the other often follows.

A media ecosystem is the system that feeds you the things you want to see. It is built around your interests, habits, and attention. The more you engage with certain kinds of media, the more that ecosystem adapts and gives you more of it.The danger in that is that it can trap people in their own way of thinking. If you are only fed ideas that support what you already believe, you stop being exposed to other perspectives. That creates a volatile environment where everyone is convinced they are right without ever being challenged by the other side.

The responsibility of the consumer is to stay curious and think independently from the media they consume. Media should be used as information, not as a replacement for personal thought. It should help inform individual opinion, not create it. That responsibility also means exposing yourself to as much perspective as possible so your understanding is shaped by more than one side.
