This hypertext project was built as a first walkthrough of the building I designed in studio. The idea was to create an interactive way of experiencing the architecture before it exists physically. It moves through the exterior, the entrance, and different spaces within the building to show the possible paths someone could take through it. Instead of presenting the building as a fixed object, the hypertext format allowed it to be explored through choice and sequence. The guitar in the background carries different moods and feelings throughout the experience and helps shape the atmosphere of each transition. Together the visuals, movement, and sound create a first impression of the architecture as an experience rather than just a design.

Making the project interactive made the building feel more tangible. Instead of viewing it through static images or drawings, the ability to move through it created a stronger sense of space and sequence. Architecture is experienced through movement, and the hypertext format allowed that to be part of the project. It gave the viewer more control over how they engaged with it and made the experience feel less like looking at a project and more like entering one. That interactivity made the design feel closer to reality and helped communicate the atmosphere in a stronger way.
