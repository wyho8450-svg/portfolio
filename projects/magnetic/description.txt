DON’T LET PERFECT BE THE ENEMY OF GOOD

Don’t let perfect be the enemy of good. This was a lesson my mentors
emphasized, and it was a standard I upheld while running Magnetic
this summer. It holds merit in the sense that spending too much time
on one task can cause you to lose sight of getting the job done in
a timely manner. It also establishes a precedent that mistakes are
allowed and can be valuable learning opportunities.
Two of the projects that came through the shop this summer were
these handrails. With the help of my interns, we completed the design
and built them to near perfection. Many interns, including myself, started
with no metal shop experience, yet this is the level of proficiency
they can achieve within just a few months of starting.
